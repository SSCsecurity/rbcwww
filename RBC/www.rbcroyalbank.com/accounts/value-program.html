          Which bank accounts are eligible for the Value Program?

          The following RBC bank accounts are eligible to be enrolled in the Value Program:

          • RBC Day to Day Banking
          • RBC Advantage Banking (includes RBC Advantage Banking for students)
          • RBC Signature No Limit Banking
          • RBC VIP Banking
          • RBC No Limit Banking (discontinued)
          • RBC Student Banking (discontinued)
          • RBC No Limit Banking for Students (discontinued)
          • RBC Day to Day Savings
          • RBC Enhanced Savings
          • RBC High Interest eSavings
          Learn more about the Value Program or explore bank accounts now.

          I just enrolled my account in the Value Program. When will I get a monthly fee rebate and RBC Rewards points, if eligible?

          You will not receive a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 in the first calendar month that you enrol your account in the Value Program. However, starting the following calendar month, your product holdings and account activities for the previous calendar month will be reviewed and if you’ve qualified for a monthly fee rebate, it will be credited to your account on your next statement. Your rebate will also be shown in the Cost Savings section of your Vantage Snapshot within the RBC Mobile app.

          You will receive any RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 you earn in the first calendar month you enrol your account within the first 5 business days of the following calendar month. For example, if you enrolled your account in the Value Program on January 15, you would receive earned RBC Rewards points within the first 5 business days in February, based on your in-store and online debit purchases from January 15 to January 31.

          Learn more about earning monthly fee rebates and RBC Rewards points through the Value Program.

          What happens if I switch from an eligible account enrolled in the Value Program to another eligible account?

          If you switch from an eligible account that is already enrolled in the Value Program to another eligible account, you do not need to enrol your new account in the Value Program—enrolment will automatically continue on your new account.

          The monthly fee and monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 (if applicable) will be pro-rated. RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 are not affected when you switch between two accounts that are eligible for the Value Program.

          How do I enrol an eligible bank account in the Value Program?

          If you are opening a new account, you can enrol it in the Value Program at the same time you open the account. If you open an account online, simply enrol in the Value Program when you are invited to do so.

          If you already have an eligible bank account, an RBC advisor can enrol your account in the Value Program for you. Please book an appointment Opens in new window, call 1-800-769-25111-800-769-2511 or visit a branch Opens in new window.

          I just enrolled my account that was previously eligible for the MultiProduct Rebate in the Value Program. When will I get a monthly fee rebate and RBC Rewards points, if eligible?

          You will not receive a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 in the first calendar month that you enrol your account in the Value Program. However, you will continue to receive the MultiProduct Rebatelegal bug 8 (if eligible) for the remainder of the calendar month.

          The following calendar month, your product holdings and account activities for the previous calendar month will be reviewed and if you’ve qualified for a monthly fee rebate, it will be credited to your account on your next statement. Your rebate will also be shown in the Cost Savings section of your Vantage Snapshot within the RBC Mobile app.

          You will receive any RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 you earn in the first month you enrol your account within the first 5 business days of the following calendar month. For example, if you enrolled your account in the Value Program on January 15, you would receive earned RBC Rewards points within the first 5 business days in February, based on your in-store and online debit purchases from January 15 to January 31.

          Learn more about earning monthly fee rebates and RBC Rewards points through the Value Program.

          Is my joint bank account eligible for the Value Program?

          Some, but not all, joint accounts are eligible. “Joint OR” accounts can be enrolled in the Value Program, provided that they have a maximum of two joint owners and both owners are 13 years of age or older. “Joint AND” accounts, “Joint OR” accounts with 3+ owners, and “Joint OR” accounts with an owner who is under age 13 are not eligible for the Value Program.

          If I change my sole-owned account to a joint account, what happens to my monthly fee rebate and RBC Rewards points earned through the Value Program?

          If you convert your sole-owned account to an eligible joint account, any sole-owned products you are using to qualify for the monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 would require consent for you to continue receiving the monthly fee rebate through the Value Program. You may not be able to receive the rebate without providing this consent. Any other products held in joint ownership with an exact name match (for example, a joint mortgage) would automatically be counted. In addition, the newly added joint owner could contribute their own individual products to the joint account—they would simply need to provide their consent.

          You will keep all of the RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 you have earned using the account and the new joint account owner will be able to contribute to earning points as well as have the ability to redeem points.

          Is matching product ownership required for joint products to count through the Value Program?

          No, matching product ownership is not required, but if there is matching ownership on an eligible product, that product will automatically be counted as a product category in the Value Program.

          Here’s how eligible product categories can be used towards an eligible joint account enrolled in the Value Program:

          • If you hold another joint product together in an eligible product category, that product will automatically be counted towards the Value Program—all names must match exactly for both the joint account and the other eligible product. The one exception is that we will need one product owner’s consent for a small business account to be counted towards the joint account, even if the names are an exact match. For example, Jon and Jane have a joint personal account, and jointly own a small business account. The business account would not be counted automatically—one of them would have to give consent first.
          • If one of you holds a product in an eligible product category individually, that product can be counted towards the Value Program—we just need that product owner’s consent. Consent to share individual products towards a joint account is “all or nothing”. This means when a product owner consents for an individual product to be counted, consent is given for all of their individual products to be counted towards the joint account.
          • If one or both of you have a small business account relationship with us (even if you jointly own the business together), that small business relationship can be counted towards the Value Program as an eligible product category. We just need one product owner’s consent (even if the name on the small business account exactly matches the name on the personal joint account).

          Note that any product holding—whether held jointly or individually—can only apply to one enrolled account in determining that account’s eligibility for a monthly fee rebate2 and earn rate for RBC Rewards points.legal bug 1

          How does the Value Program work when I have a joint account with another person?

          Both owners on a “Joint OR” account can participate in the Value Program. Each owner in a “Joint OR” account enrolled in the Value Program can:

          • Earn RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 by making debit purchases in-store or online using the enrolled account
          • View, redeem and transfer RBC Rewards points earned using the enrolled accountlegal bug 9
          • Have their eligible products be counted towards Value Program benefit determination and complete monthly account activities to qualify for the monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 on the enrolled account
          • Cancel enrolment in the Value Program at any time

          In addition:

          • If you hold a joint product together in an eligible product category, that product will automatically be counted towards the Value Program (all names must match exactly for both the joint account and the other eligible product—and there cannot be any other owners on the eligible product).
          • If one of you holds a product in an eligible product category individually, that product can be counted towards the Value Program; however, we would need that product owner’s consent. Consent to share individual products towards a joint account is “all or nothing”. This means that when a product owner consents for an individual product to be counted, consent is given for all of their individual products to be counted towards the joint account.
          • If one or both of you have a small business account relationship with us (even if you jointly own the business together), that small business relationship can be counted towards the Value Program as an eligible product category. We just need the product owner’s consent for the small business account relationship to be counted towards the joint account.
          Under a joint account, how can we use our other RBC products to count towards the Value Program?

          Here’s how eligible product categories can be used towards an eligible joint account enrolled in the Value Program:

          • If you hold another joint product together in an eligible product category, that product will automatically be counted towards the Value Program—all names must match exactly for both the joint account and the other eligible product. The one exception is that we will need one product owner’s consent for a small business account to be counted towards the joint account, even if the names are an exact match. For example, Jon and Jane have a joint personal account, and jointly own a small business account. We just need one product owner’s consent (even if the name on the small business account exactly matches the name on the personal joint account).

          Note that any product holding—whether held jointly or individually—can only apply to one enrolled account in determining that account’s eligibility for a monthly fee rebate2 and earn rate for RBC Rewards points.legal bug 1

          I am an RBC small business client. Am I eligible for the Value Program?

          You can participate in the Value Program if you have an eligible personal RBC bank account enrolled in the program. Your RBC small business account relationship can be counted as an eligible product category towards an enrolled bank account.

          • For sole personal bank accounts, your small business account will automatically be counted as a product category in the Value Program if you have linked your personal and business profiles with an advisor.
          • For joint personal bank accounts, consent must be given in order for a small business account to be counted as a product category—even if both you and your co-owner share a small business account and hold a joint personal account together. For example, Jon and Jane have a joint personal account, and jointly own a small business account. The business account would not be counted automatically—one of them would have to give consent first.
          How does product consent work for a small business relationship when I have a joint personal account enrolled in the Value Program?

          For joint personal bank accounts, consent must be given in order for a small business account to be counted as a product category—even if both you and your co-owner share a small business account and hold a joint personal account together.

          For example, Jon and Jane have a small business account together and also a joint personal bank account. Either Jon or Jane must still provide consent for the small business account relationship to be counted as a product category towards their joint personal account. If Jane provides this consent, any other individual eligible products she holds at RBC will also be counted towards the joint personal account. If, instead, Jon provides the consent, any other individual eligible products he holds at RBC will also be counted towards the joint personal account. This is because consent to share individual products towards a joint account is “all or nothing”.

          How do I link my personal and business profiles so that my small business relationship counts as a product category through the Value Program?

          An RBC advisor will need to link your personal and business profiles. To talk to an advisor, please book an appointment Opens in new window, call 1-800-769-25111-800-769-2511 or visit a branch Opens in new window.

          How do I see the RBC Rewards points I have earned through the Value Program?

          You can view your RBC Rewards points by signing in to rbcrewards.com or on the RBC Rewards applegal bug 10.

          • After signing in to rbcrewards.com, click the “Personal Rewards” drop-down menu and select “Banking Rewards”.
          • If you only have a bank account, you will see that first. If you have a bank account and an RBC Rewards credit card, the product with the most points will display first.
          • Under the “Points Activity” tab, you can see your points activity for each RBC Rewards product you have.

          You can also view your RBC Rewards points in your Vantage Snapshot within the RBC Mobile app:

          • Sign in to the RBC Mobile app.
          • Tap More.
          • Tap Vantage Snapshot. Your points will be listed under Available Points Balance.
          Why didn’t I receive a monthly fee rebate through the Value Program for my account?

          There can be several reasons why you are not seeing a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2:

          • The account is not enrolled in the Value Program.
          • The account may not be eligible for the Value Program—certain joint accounts are not eligible for the Value Program, including “Joint AND” accounts, “Joint OR” accounts with 3+ owners, and “Joint OR” accounts with an owner who is under age 13. See eligible accounts.
          • The monthly fee rebate may not have posted to your account yet—for example, a rebate earned in June will show up on your July statement.
          • You may not have enough active product categories—for example, if you recently closed a product (such as a credit card) that counted towards your eligible product categories. Learn more about eligible product categories.
          • You did not complete at least 2 out of 3 regular account activities (a pre-authorized payment, direct deposit or eligible bill payment) in a given month with the enrolled account.

          Also, if you gave consent to use a sole-owned product to meet additional product categories and earn a higher monthly fee rebate on a joint account, the consent will be taken into consideration starting the following calendar month. For example, if you gave consent in June, it will impact your monthly fee rebate in July.

          Can I combine RBC Rewards points that I have earned with a bank account enrolled in the Value Program with points I have earned with my RBC Rewards credit card?

          Yes, you can combine the RBC Rewards points you have earned using your eligible RBC bank accountlegal bug 1 (by making in-store or online debit purchases) with the points you have earned with your personal or business RBC Rewards credit card and vice versa.

          Here’s how to combine your RBC Rewards points under one points-earning product:

          • Log in to rbcrewards.com and click the “Manage Points” tab.
          • From the drop-down menu, select “Transfer Points”.
            You can transfer points between the products shown in the drop-down menu.
          • Within “Transfer Points,” you can set up a one-time transfer or an auto-transfer so that all the points you earn with your points-earning products can be pooled to one points account, making it easy to keep track of your points in one place and get you closer to your redemption goals.

          Note: Points are transferred on a 1:1 basis, meaning the number of points you transfer will not change from one points-earning product to another. In addition, there is no fee to transfer points.

          How do I see the monthly fee rebate I earned through the Value Program?

          You will be able to see the monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 you earned for a previous calendar month on the following calendar month’s account statement. For example, a rebate earned in June will show up on your July statement. Your rebate will also be shown in the Cost Savings section of your Vantage Snapshot within the RBC Mobile app.

          If you have questions about your monthly fee rebate, please book an appointment with an advisor, call 1-800-769-2511 or visit a branch.

          Could I earn RBC Rewards points at different rates if I have multiple accounts enrolled in the Value Program?

          Yes, the points earn rate may differ for each account if you have multiple accounts enrolled in the Value Program. This is because each account will be assessed individually to evaluate the number of eligible product categories you have associated with each account, which determines the rate at which your account(s) earn RBC Rewards points.legal bug 1

          For example, if you have two bank accounts enrolled in the Value Program and 5 eligible product categories:

          • One account would have 3 product categories, which means you would earn 1 RBC Rewards point for every $3 spent on debit in-store and online using the enrolled account.
          • The second account would have the remaining 2 product categories, which means you would earn 1 RBC Rewards point for every $5 spent on debit in-store and online using the enrolled account.
          What happens to my RBC Rewards points if I opt out of the Value Program before my points are awarded on the first of the month?

          RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 will be awarded based on how many points you have earned up until the day you opt out. As long as your account remains open, you will not lose the points you have previously earned when you opt out of the Value Program.

          Can I receive my monthly fee rebate or RBC Rewards points if I opt out a bank account that is enrolled in the Value Program?

          If you opt a bank account out of the Value Program, this decision will not take effect until the following calendar month. Depending on when your statement cycle date occurs, you may receive a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 (if eligible) in the month that you opt out (there are no partial monthly fee rebates).

          If you opt out the account before your statement cycle date, you will see one final monthly fee rebate (if eligible) on your statement cycle date.

          If you opt out the account after your statement cycle date, you will not see any final monthly fee rebate because it would have been awarded earlier in the month.

          For example, Jon’s account statement cycle day is on the 15th of every month. If Jon opts his account out of the Value Program on January 10, he will receive a monthly fee rebate (if eligible) on January 15. If he opts his account out of the Value Program on January 20, he already received a rebate on January 15 and will not receive an additional rebate.

          You will receive the RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 you have earned in a calendar month up until the day you opt the bank account out. In addition, as long as your account remains open, you will still be able to access and redeem your points.

          Can I receive multiple monthly fee rebates on the same bank account?

          No, you can only receive one monthly fee rebate per bank account. For example, you cannot receive both the Value Program Rebatelegal bug 2 and the Seniors Rebatelegal bug 11 on the same account in the same month. We automatically apply to your account the highest value rebate you are eligible for each month.

          If you have multiple bank accounts, you could receive a different monthly fee rebate for each account, as long as you meet the eligibility requirements to receive each of the rebates.

          If I close a product that counts as one of my eligible product categories for an account enrolled in the Value Program, what will happen to my monthly fee rebate and RBC Rewards points earn rate on my enrolled account?

          Your monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 and RBC Rewards points earn ratelegal bug 1 will not be impacted until the next calendar month, which is when we will re-evaluate whether you’re still eligible to receive a monthly fee rebate and if your points earn rate should be modified.

          How are earned RBC Rewards points affected if I return an item that I purchased on debit using my enrolled account?

          Through the Value Program, RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 are calculated monthly and take into account all in-store and online debit purchases from, and returns to, an enrolled account.

          For example, say you purchase a $300 blender on debit in December and receive your RBC Rewards points. In January, you return the blender and get a $300 credit to your bank account, but you also make $500 in debit purchases. For January, you would be awarded points for $200 of your debit spend ($500 in debit purchases minus the $300 return). Alternatively, if in January you do not make any debit purchases (or spend less than $300) and only return the $300 blender, you would not be awarded any points for January.

          I opened my personal bank account before the date on which the Value Program became available to the Canadian public (April 27, 2021). How do I determine whether the MultiProduct Rebate or Value Program is right for me?

          Will I lose my MultiProduct Rebate if I enrol in the Value Program?

          It depends. An existing bank account receiving the MultiProduct Rebatelegal bug 8 prior to the date on which the Value Program became available to the Canadian public (April 27, 2021) can continue to receive the MultiProduct Rebate as long as you continue to have the correct product holdings to qualify for it and you do not enrol that account in the Value Program.

          Here are some situations that could impact your MultiProduct Rebate:

          • If you enrol an account currently receiving the MultiProduct Rebate in the Value Program, you will permanently lose the benefit of, and any future eligibility for, the MultiProduct Rebate on that account. However, you will have the opportunity to earn a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 through the Value Program if you enrol your account in the program. In addition to earning a monthly fee rebate when you meet applicable criteria, you will also be able to earn RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 on in-store and online debit purchases.
          • If you switch from an account currently receiving the MultiProduct Rebate to an account that is not eligible for the MultiProduct Rebate (such as RBC Advantage Banking), you will permanently lose the MultiProduct Rebate on that account. However, you will have the opportunity to earn a monthly fee rebatelegal bug 2 through the Value Program if you enrol your account in the program. In addition to earning a monthly fee rebate when you meet applicable criteria, you will also be able to earn RBC Rewards pointslegal bug 1 on in-store and online debit purchases.
          • If you have more than one account—and one is receiving the MultiProduct Rebate and one is enrolled in the Value Program—eligible product categories you have will be counted towards the account(s) enrolled in the Value Program first. This means you must have enough eligible product categories to meet the criteria for both the Value Program and the MultiProduct Rebate or you will (reversibly) lose your monthly fee rebate on the account with the MultiProduct Rebate. Should you wish to restore the MultiProduct Rebate, simply opt out of the Value Program on the other account.
          Can I participate in both the MultiProduct Rebate and the Value Program at the same time?

          It is not possible to have more than one monthly fee rebate on the same bank account. However, you could participate in both the MultiProduct Rebatelegal bug 8 and the Value Program if you have two different bank accounts with RBC, where:

          • One bank account is enrolled in the Value Program, you hold at least two eligible product categories, and you do certain account activities each month (like direct deposit) in the enrolled account; and
          • The other bank account is eligible for and receives the MultiProduct Rebate by meeting its qualifying criteria (only available to bank accounts opened prior to April 27, 2021).

          For example, Jane opened an RBC Day to Day Banking account prior to April 27, 2021. She has two credit cards and two investments, so she currently benefits from the MultiProduct Rebate. Jane also opened an RBC Advantage Banking account after April 27, 2021 and enrolled this account in the program. She can earn the MultiProduct Rebate on her Day to Day Banking account and a Value Program Rebate on her Advantage Banking account

          Note that eligible product categories will be counted towards account(s) enrolled in the Value Program first. This means you must have enough eligible product categories to meet the criteria for both the Value Program and the MultiProduct Rebate or you will lose your monthly fee rebate on the account with the MultiProduct Rebate—unless you decide to opt out of the Value Program.

          For example, Jane opened an RBC Day to Day Banking account prior to April 27, 2021. She has one credit card and one investment, so she currently benefits from the MultiProduct Rebate. Jane also opened an RBC Advantage Banking account after April 27, 2021 and enrolled this account in the Value Program. Her one credit card and one investment product will be counted towards her Advantage Banking account, which means she would lose the MultiProduct Rebate on her Day to Day Banking account. That said, Jane could opt her Advantage Banking account out of the Value Program, and her product categories would be reallocated to the Day to Day Banking account, restoring her MultiProduct Rebate.
